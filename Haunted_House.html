This game was a major part of my childhood. It gave me nightmares, and I couldn't stop playing it. It's engraved in my mind, and even as an adult I still find the game to be entertaining, despite the outdated gameplay, visuals, sounds etc. I think if general audiences gave this game a try, 85% would say it was enjoyable.

What makes this game entertaining is its replay value. Even after beating the game, there is still more to do, only about half of mechanics have been used on a first run though. There is an experimental aspect to the game. Learning paterns of what items interact with different room objects. It has a single goal, which is to collect 13 keys, with all sorts of side quests, so to speak. There are hidden elements that can be unlocked by reading the books, talking with the characters in the house. Through monsters into the mix, and you hit entertainment at every level.

The game has become defunct since it has not had any updates whatsover since it's release. You cant even get the game to run, unless you know how to run windows 1993 - 1999. For a while I thought it would be great to redistribute the game and get it to run on current opperating system. I found an email for the director of the game, and sent him a message. We happy to have recieved a fan letter, and told me he would be willing to talk over zoom. We talked about his experience directing the company and working with the team. He was more than willing to share the source code, and early versions of the game. And here's where things ended up after that... A YouTuber created a chanel for posting haunted house content, and sharing secrets in the game. I was able to connect with him and share my interaction with the director. I told him I knew nothing about how to make the game run on any current platform, and he responded by sharing https://www.scummvm.org/. With the source code as reference, he could use this to rewrite the game to run on newer computers. It's been months since I've heard from him, still hoping that he can get this to work. sf

I should mention too, that I wouldn't share announce this on a public domain, but the odds that any serious fans of game would find this page are slim to say the least. So, why not.
